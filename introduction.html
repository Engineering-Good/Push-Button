Introduction¶

The aim of the EG PushButton project is to encourage autistic students to ask their teacher for help when playing with electric toys.

The EG PushButton project consists of two hardware devices:

  • Student’s button: A remotely resettable toy activation button
  • Teacher’s button: A button to remotely reset the student’s button

There’s also a companion project providing an Android app to configure the push buttons: EGPushButton Configurator

The student’s button is an easy-to-operate accessibility push button to activate an electric toy. When the student’s button is pushed, a relay is closed, powering the connected toy.

In order to encourage autistic students to ask their teacher for help, the student’s button disables itself after a random number of pushes. When the student asks for help, the teacher can then re-enable the student’s button by pushing the teacher’s button.

The two buttons communicate via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). An Android app is available to configure the push buttons.

This project was developed for Engineering Good.
